Civic mapping for the right to information and privacy in crisis

The year 2020 with Coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19) will be remembered as a special turning point separating 'Before Corona(B.C.)' and 'After Corona(A.C.).'

We had to adjust to New Normal while witnessing the limitations of the system that had functioned in previous mainstream society and seeking new ways to overcome these limitations together.

This solidarity was especially evident in online activities that leveraged 'untact' as an opportunity and affected citizens' participation in social issues, including civic hackers’ crisis mapping projects.

Overview

The adoption of contact tracing technology was especially challenged to address ethical issues studied in the field of spatial information by the nature and value of geolocation data as highly identifiable data.

These ethical questions have led to a discussion on open data justice, shedding light on the dynamics encompassing the entire open data life cycle.

Background

  • 2015

    MERS

    Middle East Respiratory Syndrome coronavirus (MERS-CoV) caused 186 confirmed cases and 38 deaths in South Korea, which was the largest number of casualties outside the Arabian Peninsula.

  • 2016

    The Revision of the Infectious Disease Control and Prevention Act

    The revised act included prompt disclosure of information such as “the movement paths, transportation means, medical treatment institutions, and contacts of patients of the infectious disease” for preventing disease infection.

  • 2019-12-31

    Patient Zero

    The outbreak of 2019 novel coronavirus disease (COVID-19) was first reported on December 31, 2019, in Wuhan, China.
