Background

  • 2015

    MERS

    Middle East Respiratory Syndrome coronavirus (MERS-CoV) caused 186 confirmed cases and 38 deaths in South Korea, which was the largest number of casualties outside the Arabian Peninsula.

  • 2016

    The Revision of the Infectious Disease Control and Prevention Act

    The revised act included prompt disclosure of information such as “the movement paths, transportation means, medical treatment institutions, and contacts of patients of the infectious disease” for preventing disease infection.

  • 2019-12-31

    Patient Zero

    The outbreak of 2019 novel coronavirus disease (COVID-19) was first reported on December 31, 2019, in Wuhan, China.

Case Analysis

Observiation of 10 crisis mapping projects (June 2020)

No map

was providing patients’ movement path information at the moment of the observation, while 7 maps had previously provided the information.

5 maps

were providing the location information of confirmed patient visit. (all maps except the maps interrupted or never provided the information)

7 maps

were interrupted either its service or information update.
4 maps closed service, 3 maps stopped updating the information.

3 maps

were still running but not providing the movement path information, while 2 maps among those maps previously supported the information.

Overall, despite the continuation of the coronavirus crisis,
many CRISIS MAPPING projects by CIVIC HACKERS did not last long for the service itself or updates, and there was a clear decrease MOVEMENT PATH data visualization even if the map service was still maintained.|

Results

Technical difficulties in data collection and processing

Technical difficulties were the decisive factor that raised barriers to sustain civic hacking projects.

Diminishing utility of location and movement data visualizations

Since the number of confirmed cases soared after the mass infection in March, civic hackers have begun to question how useful it is even if movement data is collected and visualized.

Perceived privacy concerns of location and movement visualizations

The most vulnerable group identified with disclosure of the location and movement path information of the confirmed patient's visit was local small business owners.

Just as the South Korean government learned lessons about
transparent and rapid disclosure of information from MERS,
we can learn lessons from the COVID-19 outbreak.|
